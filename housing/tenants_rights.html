North Carolina Tenants' Rights

In North Carolina, tenants have several rights, including the following:

1. Habitable Housing

Landlords are responsible for keeping the rental unit habitable. Tenants can move out if the landlord fails to do so.

2. Quiet Enjoyment

Tenants have the right to live in peace and quiet without being disturbed by their landlord or other tenants.

3. Security Deposits

Landlords must return a tenant's security deposit unless there is a valid reason to keep it.

4. Evictions

Landlords cannot remove a tenant without filing an eviction court case and receiving a judgment from a judge or magistrate.

Additional Tenant Services and Commitments

In addition to the above rights, tenants also benefit from the following services and commitments provided by management:

1. Transparent Rent Collection Process

Tenants are provided clear terms on rent due dates, payment methods, and any applicable late fees, ensuring clarity in all financial matters.

2. Timely Maintenance Response

Tenants can expect quick responses to maintenance requests, helping keep the property in good condition and resolving urgent issues efficiently.

3. Fair Tenant Screening and Equal Housing Practices

All tenants are screened fairly and consistently, adhering to equal housing laws, ensuring non-discrimination and a fair selection process.

4. Online Portal for Payments and Service Requests

Tenants gain access to an online portal to manage payments, view balances, and submit maintenance requests, providing convenience and easy communication with management.

5. Eviction Process Support

In case of any eviction matters, tenants receive support with fair processes and compliance with local laws, protecting their rights.
